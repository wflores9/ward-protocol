02
Design Rationale
WARD, stated plainly.
The wordmark is the name — four letters, maximum weight, no abstraction. WARD means protection, guardianship, custody. That meaning needs no icon, no symbol, no device. When institutional capital is at stake, you spell it out.
WARD bold. Protocol light.
The same typeface — Barlow Condensed — at opposing weights creates instant hierarchy. 900 vs 300. The protection (WARD) dominates. The category (Protocol) contextualizes. Two words. One read.
The temperature of infrastructure.
Ice blue on deep steel deliberately rejects DeFi's amber-and-neon aesthetic. This is the color of a Bloomberg terminal — a clearing system, a collateral ledger, a legal instrument. Not retail. Not consumer.
Barlow Condensed 900.
Condensed type at maximum weight has a long history in financial display — ticker boards, trading terminals, wire headlines. Letter-spacing at -2: the letters press together the way a specification should. Nothing loose.
03
Wordmark Anatomy
01 — WARD
900 Weight
Barlow Condensed at maximum weight. Font-size 172px in the base SVG. Fill #a8c5e8. The four characters form a single dense mass — the protection, stated with full force.
02 — Protocol
300 Weight
Same typeface, same size, weight 300, opacity 60%. The category contextualizes the name without competing with it. The contrast is immediate and unmistakable.
03 — Proportion
1300 : 160
ViewBox aspect ratio. The full lockup fills the width completely. Aspect ratio 8.125 : 1. Rendered via SVG — infinitely scalable with no rasterization artifacts at any size.

07
Usage Rules
✓ Always
Use the SVG wordmark — never render WARD Protocol as plain HTML text
Use an approved colorway: ice blue / gold / black on their correct backgrounds
Keep "Protocol" at weight 300 and 60% opacity relative to WARD
Pair all body copy, data, and UI labels with Space Mono
Maintain clear space equal to the cap-W height on all sides
At small sizes (below 160px rendered), use WARD letterforms only
Apply a subtle glow (drop-shadow rgba 168,197,232,0.2) on dark backgrounds
✗ Never
Don't change the weight relationship — WARD must always dominate Protocol visually
Don't stretch, skew, rotate, or alter the letterforms in any way
Don't change letter-spacing from -2 — it defines the compressed density of the mark
Don't use Georgia, serif, Inter, Roboto, or any weight other than 300/900
Don't place the mark on mid-tone, warm-tinted, or busy photographic backgrounds
Don't add outlines, shadows, fills, or effects inside the SVG element itself
Don't abbreviate to "WP" — the full lockup is always the correct representation
