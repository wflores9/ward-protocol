Protocol Architecture

WARD BUILDS. INSTITUTION SIGNS. XRPL SETTLES.

Three principals. Zero custodians. Every transaction constructed by Ward, signed by the institution, enforced by the XRP Ledger. Ward's server becomes irrelevant once the escrow is on-chain.

01 / SETUP & COVERAGE
Register & Insure

Institution registers an XLS-66 vault in the Ward compliance domain. Depositor KYC anchored on-chain via XLS-70. Coverage certificate minted as a non-transferable XLS-20 NFT — terms immutable forever.

unsigned AccountSet · NFTokenMint
02 / DEFAULT DETECTION
Detect & Confirm

WardMonitor subscribes to live XRPL WebSocket events. When health ratio drops below 1.5, three consecutive ledger closes are required before firing a verified default. No single-block manipulation possible.

WebSocket · 3-ledger window
03 / CLAIM & SETTLEMENT
Validate & Settle

ClaimValidator runs 9 deterministic checks against live XRPL state. All 9 must pass. On success, unsigned EscrowCreate returned. Institution signs. PREIMAGE-SHA-256 locks funds. NFT burns atomically on payout.

PREIMAGE-SHA-256 · NFTokenBurn
Claim Validation · F·04

NINE CHECKS. NO EXCEPTIONS.

All nine checks query live XRPL state directly. Any single failure rejects the claim with a reason code. No oracle. No off-chain data. No human override — at any step.

01Vault exists on ledgerPASS
02Policy NFT valid and owned by claimantPASS
03Policy unexpired (ledger time)PASS
04Depositor address matches NFTPASS
05Collateral ratio breached (health < 1.5)PASS
06No active escrow already pendingPASS
07KYC credential (XLS-70) valid & unexpiredPASS
08Domain credential (XLS-80) validPASS
09No duplicate claim on this NFTPASS

Deterministic by design

The same inputs always produce the same outcome. Institutional counterparties can audit the exact logic before deploying capital — no governance vote can change it after the fact.

Replay protection built in

The policy NFT burns atomically with EscrowFinish. A burned NFT fails Check 01 immediately. Double-claims are structurally impossible — not just policy-prohibited.
